Stage One Attitude Beliefs Values
Questions — What do I want? And why? And why?
→ The Root WhyWhy do I do what I do? Why do I get up in the morning? Keep asking why until you reach bedrock.
→ BeliefsWhat do I believe about myself, others, and the world? Which beliefs are serving me — and which ones are holding me back?
→ ValuesWhat do I prioritize? Where do I spend my time? Do my actions match what I say I value?
→ Gifts & StrengthsWhat are my top 3 talents? My 5 gifts? My superpower — and my kryptonite?
→ VisionIf I could see my best possible future clearly — what would it look like? Can I see it right now?
→ The Attitude CheckIs my attitude helping me see clearly — or clouding the vision I need to make a real choice?

Stage Two Focus From X to Y by When Purpose-Driven Milestones
Questions — What are the milestones that will get me there?
→ The MilestoneFrom X to Y — by when? Be honest about where you are. Be bold about where you're going.
→ Can You See It?Can you see it? Do you believe it? Will you commit to it — fully, not partially?
→ The Purpose LinkWhich part of my purpose does this objective fulfill — spiritually, emotionally, mentally, physically?
→ The FeelingWhen I imagine achieving this — what do I feel? Does that feeling strengthen my commitment or expose doubt?
→ Non-NegotiablesWhat am I not willing to compromise on — regardless of pressure, circumstances, or other people's expectations?
→ The Focus CheckWhere is my attention going right now? Is it pointed toward what I can own — or toward what I can't control?

How
How
Plans · Daily Actions · Live It
Stage Three Control 2–3 Actions Today Mastery of the Routine
Questions — What can I control? What can I do now? Today?
→ The ControllableOf everything on my plate — what can I actually control? What is mine to do today?
→ The 2–3 ThingsWhat are the 2–3 specific actions I will take today — this week — this sprint — that bring me closer to my objectives?
→ The Purpose LinkAre my daily plans actually moving me toward my purpose — or just keeping me busy?
→ The SkillWhat skill do I need to develop to perform at the level I am committing to? How am I deliberately practicing it?
→ The Energy DrainWhere am I burning energy on things I can't control? What would it free up if I let those go?
→ The Love TestDo I love what I'm doing enough to do it with full effort — even when it's hard?

When
Execute · Hear & Observe · Learn It
Stage Four 5+ to 1 Speed to Adapt Intentional Reflection
Questions — What do I notice? What feedback am I receiving?
→ 5+ WinsWhat are the 3–5 things I did well? How will I celebrate them — intentionally, not accidentally?
→ The 1 ThingIf I could do it again — what is the one thing I would do differently? What does that reveal?
→ What I NoticeWhat patterns am I seeing in my behavior, my results, my relationships? What is the data telling me?
→ Bad Moment or Bad Day?Am I responding to a moment as if it's the whole story? Is this a bad 5 minutes or a bad day?
→ The FeedbackWhat am I hearing from others that I have been reluctant to receive? What truth is hiding in the discomfort?
→ The AdjustmentDo my objectives still fit who I'm becoming — or do they need to be courageously updated?

Who
Evaluate · I Am · Leverage It
Stage Five New Identity Winning on My Terms I Know Who I Am
Questions — Who am I? What is my new identity?
→ I Am…Complete this sentence honestly and boldly: I am someone who… What words belong there now that didn't before?
→ What I KnowWhat do I now know about myself — about my strengths, my patterns, my purpose — that I didn't know when I started?
→ My Personal BenchmarksAm I hitting the goals I set for myself — on my own terms, not someone else's definition of success?
→ What I ShareWhat has this journey given me that is worth passing on? Who needs to hear my story?
→ The Legacy QuestionIf a family member described the person I've become — what would I hope they say? Are those words true yet?
→ WinningAm I facing in the right direction — and if so, am I winning? By my own definition — not anyone else's?

I
Life is a 'Tude
You have full control over your ability to choose your attitude — not your circumstances, but your response to them. This is the last and most powerful human freedom.
"If you wouldn't say that to somebody else, why say it to yourself?"
II
What You Focus on Grows
An abundance mindset sees things with greater depth and clarity. Whatever you look for, you will find. Gratitude and possibility are a choice made before results arrive.
"Where my focus goes, my energy flows."
III
Control the Controllable
Let go of what you cannot own. Illness, anxiety, and diminished performance often come from trying to control what was never yours to control. Own the process. Trust it.
"I can only control me."
