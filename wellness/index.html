Ten dimensions, one life.

The Stanford WELL for Life Scale identifies ten domains that together make up well-being. This tool borrows those ten domains as a structure for reflection — it is not the validated scale itself, and the numbers you'll give are your own subjective sense of each area, not test scores. For each domain you'll be guided through five steps: understand what it means, reflect in writing, rate how it feels, declare who you're becoming, and commit to a focused action. Click any petal to begin.

"Ten domains of well-being were identified: social connectedness, lifestyle and daily practices, physical health, stress and resilience, emotional and mental health, purpose and meaning, sense of self, financial security and satisfaction, spirituality and religiosity, and exploration and creativity." — Chrisinger et al., Stanford WELL for Life Scale (2019)
